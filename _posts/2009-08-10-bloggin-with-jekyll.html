Allright, again. But why?

I often play around with different blogs, writing my own, using different approaches. I have to admit, it's fun for me, building new sites and testing new frameworks.

For my “company” page I wrote lots of code, which had to be refactord and simplified. Writing code is good, but starting over and over from scratch, using good old rails erb first, then #haml, adding sass, using blueprint and finally yui grids. Trying out some openid, authlogic, restful-authentication stuff, is cool, but all my posts are gone and links get broken. Aaahhhwwww poor me :)

Well, it's not that important what I wrote, but somehow it would be nice to have all my posts back again (I bet they are still out there, but spread over different databases). I never backed them up, because I didn't give a Shit about it. But somehow, growing older means getting more sentimental :D So, here is my solution:

Jekyll, an awesome ruby gem, that allows me to create blog posts from my beloved texteditor. A plus on nerdfactor regarding my way of hacking stuff. No more lame textfields in browsers. And as another plus, all my posts in plain text on my favorite social coding platform github.com.

Sounds great, doesn't it? Next question is, what to use to write the actual text? textile markup doesn't feel so natural to mee. Maybe I'm going to have a look at maruku soon. Read somewhere it somehow talks to LaTeX, that would be yummi :D

That's a start :)
